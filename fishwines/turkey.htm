"Cappadoccia hasn't got as the same features as Kastamonu or Safranbolu as you described "thickly wooded valleys", but in there, there is an other symphony of sounds, people and smell. I really want to show the mystical atmosphere of those places. After you see I am sure you won't feel sorry. Just trust me."

- Nida Ozmerdivenli, our new Turkish Servas friend



After roughly a month in France with our cohousing friends, the Burke family, and a short tour of Italy, we plan to board a boat from Brindisi on the southeast coast of Italy. The boat takes us through the Greek Isles where we plan to visit for a day or two and then on to Kusadasi, Turkey. After a day or so, we'll pick up a bus bound for Keysari, a city of half a million people in Central Anatolia. In Keysari, we will be spending a few days with Nida Ozmerdivenli, a Turkish woman we have connected with through Servas. Nida is interested in traveling to Cappaddocia with us. There, we will explore the magical villages carved into the rocks. Perhaps we will find a village where we can connect with people and spend the better part of November. If that doesn't make sense, we're off to Ankara, Turkey's capital city in Central Anatolia to stay with another Servas family and explore the small towns north of Ankara.

After a month, we bus to Istanbul for a few days and then fly on to Pakistan.
