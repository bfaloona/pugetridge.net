"Todos Santos could be the end of the world or the pinnacle of a new one. At 2,500 meters above sea level, the town teeters on the edge of the Cuchumatan Mountains in northwest Guatemala, overlooking the Meso-American continent below. An old, 1960s American school bus painted vibrant colors and decorated with Catholic effigies is the only transportation that navigates the rugged dirt road winding up to the remote village."

- from an article by Koren Cappoza (http://www.horizonmag.com/5/todos-santos.asp)


Guatemala will be the first stop on our trip. We plan to be there in August 2002. We arrive in Guatemala City after a 7-hour flight from Seattle through Miami. We are planning to get outta Guatemala City quickly (the big-city capital of the county) by getting on a bus and heading for Antigua. Antigua, population 28,000 was the original capital of Guatemala until an earthquake in 1967 did significant damage. We are trying to connect with a Servas household in Antigua in order to get on our feet and spend a few days enjoying this small city. From there we will take a five-hour bus ride to Huehuetenango in the Western Highlands where we have another Servas connection. After a night in Huehuetenango, back on the bus for the bumpy one hour ride to Todos Santos Cuchumatan. This village of 2,000 is home to mostly Mayan people who speak primarily Mam (a traditional Mayan language). Todos is supposed to have three Spanish/Mam language schools that set people up to stay with villagers and participate in a one-on-one (or in our case a one-on-three) language emersion program. We're still working on trying to connect with any one of these three schools.
