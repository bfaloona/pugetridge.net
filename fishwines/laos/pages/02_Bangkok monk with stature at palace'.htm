Bangkok monk with statue at palace
Buddhist men are expected to become monks at some time in their life; it’s considered a responsibility and honor to the family. Monks wear robes of orange, follow a set of religions “precepts” and walk the streets each morning collecting offerings of food from townspeople. Many young men don the robes for only a month or two, others for several years or a lifetime.
