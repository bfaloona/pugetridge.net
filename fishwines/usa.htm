"Hello from our home: Unit B-5, Puget Ridge Cohousing, Delridge community, West Seattle Peninsula, Seattle, Washington, USA."

 

 

As we pack up our lives and prepare to leave on this yearlong journey, our minds are swirling. "Did we plan enough?" "Will we do well together?" And the big: "What will it be like?" None of us know.

Barbara has traveled through Europe for 4 months about 30 years ago and we've done a few weeklong family vacations but…a year?

We're excited, we're scared and we are walking humbly out of the courtyard, leaving our home, our cat "Climber" (thanks Joe and Kathryn) and Paul's job (thanks Jim, Kate, Gina, Philippa, Ty, Parie, Glenn, and Raeanne) in good hands and our futures a bit uncertain.

The defining feature of our lives these days seems to be the speed at which we live it. American culture is moving fast. There's no shortage of great relationships, parties, events, exciting endeavors at work, etc. but it seems there is precious little time in between to savor these moments, to lock them into our consciousness or to explore their deeper meaning. We would all like this to change as we step onto the plane for Guatemala and then to bring a slower speed with us into our daily lives.

We hope you enjoy previewing our trip and following along as we go.

Take care and much love.

- Barbara, Paul and Annie
