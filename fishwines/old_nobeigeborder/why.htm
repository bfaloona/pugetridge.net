Why Are We Doing This?

 

 

I am going on this trip for a couple of reasons. The first reason that comes to mind is my parents want to, not that I 'm not looking forward to it. I think it will be lots of fun. But it wasn't really my idea. It sounds like great fun to travel the world but I will really miss my friends and family while I am gone.

I really want to improve my social skills, here's my chance. My second reason is, as I travel around so much I will have to make friends fast and I think that will help me be more proud of how I am and more willing to be myself.

The last reason is I think it will be really fun to see different cultures and different life styles I hope I gain a better understanding of the world around me. Our one side view is not enough for me.

- Annie


Why are we doing this, I ask myself. Our life is very good here I love Seattle, I love the community we live in, I love my dear friends, I love Paul and Annie, my little not-so-nuclear family and my extended family, I love the opportunity I have to teach at the UW. Things are very good in my life - good enough to take on a new adventure, to open myself up to new countries, new people, new places in myself.

I am curious about the world we live in. I want to experience the rich fabric of people and places, taste their foods, hear their poetry, sleep in their houses, share their friendship. I want to see how the world's people are inhabiting the earth, how they come together and support each other in community, how they build their buildings in tune with their environment, how they tell the stories of their land, how they celebrate their joy and their sadness, how they live out their lives on the web of this planet.

As I set out on this trip I am in touch with the preciousness of the people and places in my life. The beauty of my life sits tenderly with me right now. I hope that I carry the tenderness with me, that it creates poetry of my travels.

- Barbara


I've never really been into traveling - I haven't much.

For the last seven years, I've been engaged in creating community both at work and at home. For myself, it's been about trying to create a place where I feel a deep sense of belonging despite the fact that it often feels like I'm living in an alien culture. The forces of materialism and our fast-paced global "Mc e-world" are strong. How can we establish an enduring cultural practice that keeps these forces from engulfing our lives? With my wife, daughter, parents, neighbors and several close friends; we have done a pretty good job. We have strong relationships, we share our lives with our neighbors, have jobs that matter to us, continue to expand our "selves" and love each other. But I don't want us to stop there.

I want to see how other people around the world spend their days, juggle the demands of work and family life and reach for meaning and happiness in it all. I want to see what we, the human community, have (and don't have) in common. And I want to broaden my view of what's possible so that we might find ways of deepening our connections to each other and to our home.

I'm also very excited about what this trip will be for our family. A year of being together in this way will challenge us as well as bringing us closer.

- Paul
