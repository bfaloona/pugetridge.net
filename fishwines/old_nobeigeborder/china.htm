I don't know what to say about China. It is a mystery to me. We've chosen China because of its importance in the world's human community and the boldness with which it is taking up the challenges of population growth and economic development. The picture I get is of a rapidly changing people, mixing very old with very new, clinging fiercely to traditions while running headlong into modernity. We expect this to be one of the defining features of our visit there.


 

After crossing the border from Laos into Yunan province in southern China, we plan to travel over land to Kunming a rapidly modernizing city of about 3.5 million people. We may have an English speaking contact there, through the relative of a neighborhood friend, to receive us and show us around. From Kunming, we will travel to LiJiang, a City that appears to be an uneasy mix of old and new. The old, a world heritage site built over 600 years ago along a series of canals that are still used for drinking, bathing, laundry, etc. and the fast growing, 43 year old new part sprawling outward. We hope to make friends and settle in for several weeks before working our way down to Hong Kong and a flight to our last stop - Japan.
