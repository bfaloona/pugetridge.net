Over the past few years, I've (Paul) been involved with the residents of High Point...


 

...a community of 700 families living in public housing owned by the Seattle Housing Authority. The majority of these families are immigrant families, many from Southeast Asia (Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia…) and the Horn of Africa (Somalia, Ethiopia, Eritrea). During this time I had the privilege of working with Tigabie Tekeba, an employee of the Seattle Housing Authority and an immigrant from Ethiopia.

Working with Tigabie has made a huge impression on me. His integrity, his openness and sense of fairness for all people are rare indeed. We have become good friends, taking turns taking each other to lunch and all the while talking about all aspect of our lives. Through these encounters and through working with other Ethiopian immigrants, I have become very interested in seeing this land, one of the poorest nations in the world and how people live.

We are hoping to have a chance to visit with some of Tigabie's relatives in a farming village in the north of Ethiopia in the Gondar region.
