Pakistan Journals



Friday, January 10th - Nairobi, Kenya

Throughout our visit with our friends in Pakistan, we did a lot of playing, visiting and relaxing - definitely a recharge. We did very little journaling. As Annie put it, "who wants to journal when you can play with kids your age!" No doubt our sights and experiences will work their way into our thoughts as we continue on in the trip.

But, here are some of the things we did in Karachi:

  • Sang 60's songs on the beach with Akiel's incredible repertoire.

  • Rode a camel on New Year's day.

  • Taught Lali, Rahul and Ravi to play backgammon.

  • Lost to Rahul in chess - twice.

  • Went to a wedding reception dressed in Lali and Khalid's clothes.

  • Had clothes custom made.

  • Bought a little handmade sort of violin, stocked up on books in English for Annie and looked for Rahul in the Sunday market.

  • Saw lots of drummers dressed in yellow by the side of the road hoping for a gig.

  • Felt pain and sadness saying "no" to so many beggars of all types - children, amputees, Afghan refugees, transvestites…

  • Made up a song.

  • Went sailing and motor boating in the very stinky bay of the Arabian Sea.

  • Saw donkeys, camels, cats, cows, monkeys, parrots, hawks and crows sharing the Karachi streets with cars, motorcycles, and amazingly colorful decorated busses.

  • Had wonderful food cooked by "Bua" at Lali and Khalid's.

  • Stayed up till 2 am telling stupid jokes (and a few good ones) but laughed anyway.

  • Had henna done by Lali's niece

  • Saw a new community of 1600 poor families in the barren landscape north of Karachi.

  • Played basketball and led a basketball clinic after school for a few middle schoolers at Ravi and Rahul's school.

  • Went to the Shiny new mall and bought some great Pakistani music CD's.

  • Took a car ride snaking our way through the "old city" of Karachi with its colonial architecture and its crowded streets.

  • Saw Lali and Khalid's new house and marveled at the custom craftsmanship - we even gave them a few design ideas.

  • Stayed up till 1:30 in the morning jamming with AJ on keyboard, Rahul on drums, Ravi on flute and Paul on guitar - it actually sounded good! No, really!

  • Had dinner out and the girls were given free flowers.

  • Did all of our laundry!

  • Buried Khalid's leg and went swimming with the jellyfish.

  • Slept late almost every day (except for Annie).

  • Got hair trimmed.

  • Stayed up way past midnight watching the tape of Rahul's 3rd birthday until the tape broke.

  • Visited Lali and Khalid's work places.

  • Got stuck in a number of traffic jams.

  • Saw a few beautiful sunsets.
