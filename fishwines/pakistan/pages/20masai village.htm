Kenya Pictures, Jan. 2003 / 20masai village

1/25/2003

Before entering Masai Mara reserve, a huge wildlife refuge that straddles Kenya and Tanzania and encompasses the Great Rift Valley and the Serengeti Plain, our group had a brief stop at a Masai village. It was a strange combination of living their traditional life and accommodating tourists. Many Masai people still live in many small villages (this one had 82 people). With the creation of the park, much of the land the Masai used for grazing and building villages (they are nomadic and move every 6-8 years) was locked up. That and the influence of the tourist economy are bringing considerable change to the Masai people.
